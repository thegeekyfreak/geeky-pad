A Journey – From Champak-Van to Hogwarts

Food Travel

Most of my friends today know me as the bookworm, always reading kinda guy. But to be honest I wasn't this way always. Trust me, every avid reader has an interesting story for how they fell into this ocean.

For me it begins back when I was in class 3rd or 4th I guess, when I wasn't so interested into reading stuff, at most I'd see those Garfield, Calvin n Hobbes comic strips they issued in newspapers. Now our (I'm saying our because my brother too was dragged into this) father suddenly got too enthusiastic about this thought that reading is good for academics and your vocabulary (yes it is…) and he told us to read newspaper articles and stuff. And to make things serious, we were asked questions from the articles we read to check if we really did it or not…

This went on for awhile when our reading text was promoted to "Champak"…(brings back ol' memories?)… But the reality was that our heart wasn't really I to it. To us, it was no more than the textbooks that we read to get through our exams. But maybe the bookworm had laid it eggs somewhere, cause now I got this hang of reading my literature books for a past-time. Now we come to class 5th when we were allowed to get books issued by our library too…

And there was a variety of students who got them issued –

  • Those who'd go for heavy encyclopedias (mostly to show off their intellect)
  • Those who tried to seem funk by taking books like Goosebumps (heck if they ever even read a page of it…)
  • Those who stick with simple choices of their own (like me)
  • And those who went to copy the first two types

I started with Nandan & other classics (Huckleberry Finn, Oliver Twist, etc) and then moved on to Tinkle (me n my brother made a habit of buying a book from the bookstalls at railway station, every trip we had)… then Nagraj (those who don't know, he's our true indian comic book hero… Doga, Commander Dhruv etc are also there whom unfortunately we nowadays don't give much recognition)…

This carried on for a while, till I got the hang of novels (this one too started as a task, but it was only me this time; the ones I began with were those heavy personality development books like Robin Sharma, etc which seemed too boring to me).

Turning point was when I got The Diary Of A Wimpy Kid… oh boy that kid… (to those who relate themselves with Nobita, trust me, this boy would describe your life's story. This book has kept me hooked till date, not only to read novels but inspired me to keep a diary to pour out your crazy endeavors as well…

There was a time when my parents had to force me to read… And now it's time when I've to hear a lot for this much reading… lol

Talking about books how could one forget Harry Potter; whose world of magic spellbounded your belief in the wonders which might be hidden somewhere, that curiosity of searching, or finding yourself amongst the fantastic beasts, seeing yourself in your favorite house at Hogwarts (I'm a Gryffindor… just telling). Even an imagination such beautiful, an illusion of our minds so captivating; that we could live in such a dream and wish it never breaks…

Or Dan Brown; whose Robert Langdon inspired me to stay curious about the details and seeing patterns everywhere, you never know what mystery you may uncover someday.

Last book I read was more than an year ago, the long awaited, 'War of Lanka', by Amish Tripathi , it's a good read too I'd say. Every book I've read has taught me something. And if I came to words to express that, it'd never end. So I'd put my pen down here… and with that, keep reading guys and evolve your hidden conscience !! Peace…✌✌
